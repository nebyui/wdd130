Principles

The principles of art are "rules" that each component or object that makes up an art piece follow.

Anatomy

When drawing people or animals (or plants too!), it is key to understand the anatomical structers of said people or animals. It will help with creating the right shapes and preportions of the body, making them appear more realistic. Primarily focus on the muscle and skeletal systems, as those directly correlate to the preportions and shapes of the parts of the body.

Perspective

Perspective is how objects appear to the viewer or "camera" of a piece to give the impression their size, position, and depth relative to each other. This is important to making objects appear 3D on a 2D medium. For example, objects further away appearing smaller to the viewer is one aspect of perspective.

Form

Form is the shape and occupied space of an object, both in the 3D enviorment and the 2D image.

Lighting

Understanding how light works is key to making images look realistic and adds depth to a 2D image. All light comes from a light source, and the position the light source in the space affects where the highlights and shadows appear on a object.

Color Theory

Colors choice is important for creating realism, effects, or setting a tone to the piece. Color has three components:

Hue: The shade of visible color (red, blue, green, yellow, purple, etc, and all shades in between)
Saturation: The intensity of the color. High saturation makes the color more bright and vibrant, whereas low saturation looks greyish and dull.
Value: How light or dark the color is. In other words, it is how close the color is to pure white or pure black.

Composition

Composition is the way all of the principles and elements are combined and organized in the completed image. Thinking about composition means thinking about where the viewer's eye is guided, the balance and harmony of the different elements, how to convey the the message or intent of the piece, etc. Composition has its own set of principles.

The visual balance of the piece. This is how the components are distributed in the piece. There are different kinds of balance, such as symmetrical and asymmetrical.

The visual movement or flow of the piece. This can lead the viewers eye or add a sense of motion.

Establishing the focal point or making certain components stand out against others. Contrast, scale, and placement within the image is important with this and helps with communicating the main message of the piece.

The level of variation in colors, values. and textures. Contrast helps with making components stand out or to help convey the meaning of the piece.

Maintaining the proper sizes and proportions of the objects in the piece. Perspective plays a significant role in this and it is good to make sure the preportions appear accurate.

The sense of togetherness that the different components create. Good unity means that the components and elements work together rather than appearing random and disjointed

The repeated use of a certain component or element. This can be used to create the rhythm, movement, and/or unity, or to simply make a pattern.
